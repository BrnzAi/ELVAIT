1. Scope and Purpose

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") supplements the ELVAIT Terms of Service and governs the processing of personal data by the Processor on behalf of the Controller when the Controller uses the ELVAIT platform at elvait.ai (the "Service").

ELVAIT is an AI decision-support SaaS platform that runs surveys collecting stakeholder input for AI investment decisions. The Processor processes personal data solely to provide the Service as instructed by the Controller.

2. Definitions

Terms not defined herein have the meaning given in the EU General Data Protection Regulation 2016/679 ("GDPR"). "Personal Data," "Data Subject," "Processing," "Controller," "Processor," and "Sub-processor" have the meanings set out in Article 4 GDPR.

4. Obligations of the Processor

  1. Instructions. The Processor shall process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller, including with regard to transfers to third countries, unless required by EU or Member State law. The Processor shall immediately inform the Controller if, in the Processor's opinion, an instruction infringes the GDPR.
  2. Confidentiality. The Processor shall ensure that persons authorised to process Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality.
  3. Security. The Processor shall implement the technical and organisational measures described in the Technical and Organisational Measures (TOMs) annex, ensuring a level of security appropriate to the risk.
  4. Sub-processing. The Processor shall not engage another processor without prior general written authorisation of the Controller. The Controller hereby grants general authorisation for the sub-processors listed in Section 7. The Processor shall inform the Controller of any intended changes and provide the Controller an opportunity to object.
  5. Data Subject Rights. The Processor shall assist the Controller, by appropriate technical and organisational measures, in fulfilling the Controller's obligation to respond to data subject requests under Chapter III GDPR.
  6. Assistance. The Processor shall assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with Articles 32–36 GDPR, taking into account the nature of processing and information available to the Processor.
  7. Deletion and Return. At the choice of the Controller, the Processor shall delete or return all Personal Data after the end of the provision of the Service and delete existing copies unless EU or Member State law requires storage.
  8. Audit. The Processor shall make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with Article 28 GDPR and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller. The Processor shall immediately inform the Controller if an audit request, in the Processor's opinion, infringes the GDPR or other applicable law.

5. Obligations of the Controller

The Controller warrants that:

  • It has a lawful basis for the processing of Personal Data under the GDPR;
  • It has provided appropriate notice to data subjects regarding the use of the Service;
  • It shall not use the Service to process special categories of personal data (Article 9 GDPR) or data relating to criminal convictions (Article 10 GDPR);
  • Its instructions to the Processor comply with applicable data protection law.

6. Data Location

All Personal Data is stored and processed within the European Union, specifically in the Google Cloud europe-west1 (Belgium) region. The Processor shall not transfer Personal Data outside the EU/EEA without the Controller's prior written consent and without appropriate safeguards under Chapter V GDPR (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses adopted by the European Commission).

8. Data Breach Notification

The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay, and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting the Controller's Personal Data. The notification shall include:

  • A description of the nature of the breach, including the categories and approximate number of data subjects and records affected;
  • The name and contact details of the Processor's data protection contact;
  • A description of the likely consequences of the breach;
  • A description of the measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate its effects.

9. Data Retention and Deletion

  • Active accounts: Personal Data is retained for the duration of the Controller's subscription.
  • Account deletion: Upon the Controller's request or termination of the Service, the Processor shall delete all Personal Data within 30 days, unless retention is required by applicable law.
  • Backups: Automated database backups are retained for a maximum of 30 days and are then permanently deleted.
  • The Processor shall provide written confirmation of deletion upon the Controller's request.

10. International Transfers

Where Personal Data is transferred to a sub-processor outside the EU/EEA (e.g., Resend, Inc.), such transfer shall be subject to appropriate safeguards, including the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses (Module 3: Processor to Sub-processor) as adopted by Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914, or any successor mechanism approved under GDPR.

11. Liability

Each party's liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations set out in the ELVAIT Terms of Service, except that neither party's liability for breaches of its GDPR obligations shall be limited to the extent prohibited by applicable law.

12. Term and Termination

This DPA takes effect upon the Controller's acceptance of the ELVAIT Terms of Service and remains in force for the duration of the Processor's processing of Personal Data on behalf of the Controller. Obligations that by their nature should survive termination (including Sections 7, 8, 9, and 11) shall survive.

13. Governing Law

This DPA shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Federal Republic of Germany, without regard to conflict-of-law principles. The courts of Munich, Germany shall have exclusive jurisdiction.

14. Amendments

The Processor may update this DPA to reflect changes in law or the Service. Material changes will be communicated to the Controller with at least 30 days' notice. Continued use of the Service after the effective date of changes constitutes acceptance.
